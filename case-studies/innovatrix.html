01 — Overview

What this project set out to do

Innovatrix Teams LTD needed a live multi-page website that could sell the agency to startups and SMEs: UI/UX, software development, branding, and digital transformation — without looking like another template. The site had to earn trust in seconds, make services obvious, and turn curiosity into “Let’s Talk.”

Scope

Home, About, Services, and Contact — plus careers cues and shared brand chrome.

My role

End-to-end UI/UX: research synthesis, IA, wireframes, visual system, and hi-fi UI.

Outcome

A shipped live site with clear CTAs, stronger service hierarchy, and post-launch KPIs.

02 — Problem

The website wasn’t doing the selling

Before redesign, the story was fragmented. Prospects couldn’t tell what Innovatrix actually delivered, proof lived too far down the page, and Contact felt like an afterthought — so serious leads bounced or messaged socials instead of completing the form.

  • Value buried. Headlines were generic; differentiation vs other Abuja agencies wasn’t clear in 5 seconds.
  • Services hard to scan. Offerings blended together — founders couldn’t map their need to a package.
  • Weak conversion path. Multiple competing CTAs, little social proof near primary actions, Contact form friction.
  • Brand inconsistency. Typography and accent colors shifted page to page, so the company felt smaller than the work.

03 — Approach

How I approached it — and why

I treated the site like a product funnel, not a brochure. Research first, then a strict information hierarchy, then visual brand once the structure held.

01

Talk to decision-makers

Interviewed and surveyed founders / marketing leads who’d hired (or almost hired) agencies. Their language shaped heroes and FAQ, not ours.

02

Map jobs-to-be-done

Three jobs: hire an agency, understand culture/team, get in touch fast. Every page had to serve one primary job first.

03

Design for skim, then depth

Proof and CTA above the fold; long-scroll storytelling (blogs, FAQ, mission) only after trust is earned. That’s why Home leads with rating + talent cards.

04

Test the risky bits

Usability sessions on hero comprehension, Services card hierarchy, and Contact form completion before locking hi-fi.

04 — Design process

From research to live launch

A six-step loop with check-ins after wireframes and after usable hi-fi.

  1. DiscoverInterviews, competitor audit, analytics review
  2. DefinePersonas, problem framing, success metrics
  3. IdeateSitemap, content outline, CTA strategy
  4. DesignWireframes → design system → hi-fi pages
  5. ValidateQuestionnaire follow-ups + usability tests
  6. ShipBuild handoff, QA, launch, KPI tracking

08 — Usability testing

Five moderated sessions before launch

Remote think-aloud tests with founders and marketers on clickable Figma prototypes of Home, Services, and Contact. Tasks timed; findings fixed before handoff.

Task 1

“Find how Innovatrix could help with a product redesign and start a conversation.”

4/5 completed via Services → Contact in under 45s after CTA clarity fix.

Task 2

“What does this company specialize in? Tell me in your own words.”

Before: only 2/5 named UI/UX + software. After: 5/5 mentioned design + build.

Key fixes

  • Moved rating + talent cards higher on Home
  • Dark primary Services card for App Development hierarchy
  • Reduced Contact form fields; email always visible beside form

10 — Solution

A conversion-first site with a cohesive brand system.

The final UI keeps Home loud and proof-heavy, Services scannable, About human, and Contact low-friction — all locked to Funnel Display, Onest, and the purple / lime language below.

Home

Landing that sells design & tech in one glance

Hero messaging, 3D visual cards, trust logos, services, testimonials, blogs, and FAQ - sequenced for scroll and conversion.

About

11 — Live KPIs

What we measure since launch

The site is live, so success isn’t a Figma export — it’s whether the right people reach out and how cleanly they move through the funnel.

+38%Contact form submits (first 6 weeks vs. prior month)
−22%Bounce rate on Home after hero + proof reorder
41sMedian time to first primary CTA click
4.6 / 5Avg clarity score in post-launch feedback (n=11)

Reflection

What I learned

  • Prospects skim ruthlessly. In tests, people decided “relevant or not” before reading body copy — so Services labels and proof had to be visual, not paragraph-based.
  • Contact friction kills warm intent. Cutting optional fields and keeping email visible beside the form mattered more than another purple accent.
  • Careers users take a different door. Founders never opened job listings; designers went straight to About. One nav had to serve two mental models without clutter.
  • Live metrics close the loop. Shipping wasn’t the finish — form submits and bounce rate showed which hero/proof combo actually converted.
