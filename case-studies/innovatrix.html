Introduction

A conversion-driven agency site that feels premium and clear.

Innovatrix Teams needed a digital presence that matched their work - UI/UX, software development, branding, and transformation for startups and businesses. I designed a multi-page experience across Home, About, Services, and Contact that leads with bold typography, purple accents, and a path to "Let's Talk."

01 - Objectives

Design goals

Every page had to sell credibility fast, surface services without fluff, and make it easy to start a conversation.

Goal 01

Lead with clarity

Open with conversion-driven headlines and proof - ratings, team trust, and a clear primary CTA - before diving into long copy.

Goal 02

Show the offer

Make Services scannable: app development, UI/UX design, and consultancy with a dark highlight card for hierarchy.

Goal 03

Build brand cohesion

Unify navy, lavender, lime, and rounded imagery across Home, About, Services, and Contact so the site feels like one product.

Goal 04

Close the loop

Contact should feel approachable - details, socials, office, and a form that invites collaboration without friction.

Home

Landing that sells design & tech in one glance

Hero messaging, 3D visual cards, trust logos, services, testimonials, blogs, and FAQ - sequenced for scroll and conversion.

About

Mission, culture, and careers in one story

Conversion-driven design messaging, mission overlay, stats, purpose-driven service cards, team portraiture, and open roles.

Services

What they deliver - designed to scan

A hero with people + brand stamp, three service cards, a stand-out grid, and a soft mid-page CTA into the shared dark footer.

Contact

Build together - details, form, and office

Split layout with call/email details, floating form card, social follow row, Abuja office, and the signature "Let's Work Together" footer.

Brand system

Navy + lavender + lime

A high-contrast agency palette: deep navy for authority, lavender for CTAs and emphasis, lime for energy moments, and generous rounded surfaces throughout.

Navy #050517 Lavender #A37BFF Lime #C1FF72 White

Reflection

What I learned

  • Agency sites sell trust first. Ratings, team faces, and partner logos do more heavy lifting than long feature dumps.
  • One palette, many moods. Navy, lavender, and lime kept Home playful and Contact calm without feeling like different brands.
  • CTAs need a rhythm. Repeating "Let's Talk / Let's Work Together" at section ends kept conversion optionality without shouting.
  • Services benefit from contrast. One dark card among light ones instantly signals the primary offer in the Services grid.
