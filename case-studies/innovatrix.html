01 - Overview

Building a digital front door for a design-led agency

Innovatrix Teams LTD had strong delivery and referral momentum, but no owned website to convert cold interest. The brief was a greenfield multi-page experience that could sell UI/UX, software development, branding, and digital transformation without reading like another agency template.

I led end-to-end UI/UX: stakeholder interviews, survey synthesis, information architecture, lo-fi structure, visual system, hi-fi UI, and pre-launch validation. The goal was simple to state and hard to execute - earn trust in the first scroll, make services scannable, and turn curiosity into a conversation.

02 - Problem, approach, solution

From no website to a conversion-ready presence

Problem

No owned site to convert interest

Leads came through referrals, WhatsApp, and social DMs. That worked for warm intros, but cold prospects had nowhere credible to evaluate services, team, or how to start.

  • No Home to Services to Contact funnel
  • Hard to prove design + build fit in one place
  • Brand looked smaller than the work on social alone

Approach

Research first, then structure, then polish

I treated the site like a product funnel: talk to decision-makers, map jobs-to-be-done, design for skim-then-depth, and test the risky bits before locking hi-fi.

  • Survey + interviews shaped hero and Services IA
  • Three primary jobs: hire, understand culture, contact
  • Proof and CTA above the fold; depth after trust

Solution

A live, conversion-first multi-page site

One shared nav, footer, and visual system across Home, About, Services, Company, and Contact. Home leads with proof and CTA, Services scans with clear hierarchy, About carries culture and careers, and Contact stays low-friction.

Reflection

What I learned

  • Prospects skim ruthlessly. In tests, people decided “relevant or not” before reading body copy, so Services labels and proof had to be visual, not paragraph-based.
  • Contact friction kills warm intent. Cutting optional fields and keeping email visible beside the form mattered more than another purple accent.
  • Careers users take a different door. Founders never opened job listings; designers went straight to About. One nav had to serve two mental models without clutter.
  • Live metrics close the loop. Building from zero meant launch wasn’t the finish - form submits, bounce rate, and clarity scores showed whether the funnel actually worked.
